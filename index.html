2022-09 to 2025-06 | Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST)

Mechanisms of the photoreceptor LITE-1 mediating the response to high-temperature stress in C. elegans.

LITE-1, an efficient short-wavelength light sensor protein in C. elegans, and its homolog, GR28BD, in Drosophila, have been reported to sense both light and temperature. Using behavioral analysis, genetics, and in vivo calcium imaging, we have preliminarily identified that the lite-1 mediated heat stress response in C. elegans play a role in the ASH, ASI, and AWB neurons. Based on transcriptome sequencing, we focused downstream on the MAPK and DAF-16/FOXO pathways... ...

2018-09 to 2022-06 | China University of Geosciences (CUG)

Microbial adaptation in extremely acidic environments, with a focus on mobile genetic elements.

Acidophiles, as a category of primary extremophiles with superior survival and oxidation ability, are widely distributed in acid mine drainage (AMD) environments. The limited genome information has kept them from being studied on molecular taxonomy and environmental adaptation in depth. In my previous research, I utilized bioinformatic analyses to reveal the potential roles of various mobile genetic elements (MGEs), including insertion sequences (IS), clustered regularly interspaced short palindromic repeats (CRISPR), prophages, and genomic islands (GIs). This work contributed to our understanding of genomic plasticity and evolutionary adaptation mechanisms in extreme AMD environments.

Based on this project, under the meticulous guidance of Liyuan Ma professor, I led team to participate in the 2022 China Undergraduate Life Sciences Contest, where we won the first prize. Attached is a link to the competition's online video (5min).
